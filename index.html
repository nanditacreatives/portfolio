Point of View

What I believe about
B2B content in 2026

The job asks for strong opinions. Here they are — unfiltered.

01

Generic AI content is the death of brand, not a shortcut to volume

The failure mode of AI-generated content isn't quantity — it's that it sounds like nobody. The job is feeding models the right raw material: real customer language, actual POVs, specific examples. Prompting from nothing gives you nothing.

02

SEO without topical authority is a leaky bucket

Publishing 50 blog posts across 20 topics doesn't win search in 2026. Depth in fewer categories, tight internal linking, and content that earns AI overview citations — that's what moves organic. Spray-and-pray is dead.

03

Sales assets fail because content people don't sit in sales calls

Most sales enablement content is built from assumptions, not actual objections. The best proof assets I've seen come from someone who listened to 20 sales calls, identified the three questions that stall every deal, and answered those specifically.

04

Content pipelines are product problems

A content team without a system is just a collection of one-off projects. The infrastructure matters as much as the output — prompt libraries, brand voice docs, brief templates, measurement dashboards. Without them, quality doesn't scale.

05

Creative volume is a competitive moat — if the bar is right

The teams winning paid social test 30 creative variants per concept, not 3. But volume without an editorial bar produces noise. The goal is building a machine that can move fast without losing taste.

06

The best B2B content is written for one person, not a segment

The content that moves pipeline reads like it was written for the person opening it. That's not a style choice — it comes from doing enough customer interviews that you know exactly what they say to their boss when they're frustrated.

Content Systems

Strategy, not just posts

Building content that scales requires infrastructure. Here's what that looks like when I run it.

60-Day Instagram Content Series — Full Production Bible

Content Strategy · System Design

Before posting a single reel in a 60-day series, I built a complete production bible: 28+ days of content planned to the shot level, with per-day concept rationale, shot lists, voiceover scripts, edit notes, caption copy, hashtag strategy, and CTAs — all tied to a batch-shoot schedule designed to maximize output per filming session.

"Batch shoot Sunday evening + Monday morning. Four of the seven reels can be filmed in two sessions if you plan the locations in advance."

Every content decision in the bible is explained — not just what to post, but why that angle, why that format, and what the failure mode looks like. Hook types are varied deliberately across the week. Engagement tactics (comment prompts, A/B voting, transparency plays) are rotated to test what drives interaction.
